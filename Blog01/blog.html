Discovering Hidden Gems in Kyoto

A journey through the lesser-known temples and gardens of Japan's ancient capital

Published: May 15, 2023 Category: Travel Reading Time: 8 min

Kyoto, the heart of traditional Japan, is famous for its golden pavilions and bamboo forests. But beyond the well-trodden tourist paths lie secret gardens, quiet temples, and neighborhoods where time seems to stand still. Join me as I explore these hidden treasures.

The Serenity of Shisen-dō

My journey began in the northeastern hills of Kyoto, at Shisen-dō (詩仙堂), the "Hall of Poetic Immortals." Unlike the crowded Kinkaku-ji or Fushimi Inari, this 17th-century hermitage receives few visitors, allowing for moments of true contemplation.

The moss garden at Shisen-dō, designed to represent waves on the ocean

The garden, designed by Ishikawa Jōzan, a retired samurai and scholar, is a masterpiece of borrowed scenery (shakkei). The carefully placed maple trees frame the distant Higashiyama mountains, making them appear as part of the garden itself. As I sat on the veranda, the only sounds were the rustling leaves and the occasional chime of a temple bell.

"In Kyoto, beauty hides in plain sight. You must learn to slow down, to look between the obvious landmarks, to hear the silence between the sounds."

Practical Tips for Visiting

  • Visit in late November for the best autumn colors, or in June during the rainy season when the moss is at its greenest.
  • The temple opens at 9am - arriving early means you might have the place to yourself.
  • Don't miss the small tea house where you can enjoy matcha while overlooking the garden (¥500).

Exploring the Philosopher's Path

No visit to Kyoto is complete without walking the Philosopher's Path (Tetsugaku-no-michi), but most guidebooks will direct you to the cherry blossom-lined section near Ginkaku-ji. Few venture further south where the path becomes quieter, passing by lesser-known temples and artisan workshops.

About halfway along the path, I discovered a tiny soba noodle shop run by an elderly couple. Their handmade buckwheat noodles, served with a view of a miniature waterfall in their garden, became my favorite lunch spot in Kyoto.

Video: Morning Rituals at a Hidden Temple

One of my most memorable experiences was being invited to observe the morning rituals at a small Zen temple not listed in any guidebooks. The head priest, who spoke some English, explained the significance of each gesture and chant.

Final Thoughts

Kyoto rewards those who wander without rigid itineraries. By stepping away from the "must-see" lists and allowing time for unexpected discoveries, I found a Kyoto that felt more authentic and personal. The city's true magic lies not just in its famous landmarks, but in the quiet moments between them.

More Hidden Gems to Explore

  1. Ōtagi Nenbutsu-ji - A temple with 1,200 whimsical rakan statues
  2. Hōnen-in - A thatched-roof temple with beautiful moss gardens
  3. Shōren-in - Known as the "Awadaguchi Palace" with stunning night illuminations
